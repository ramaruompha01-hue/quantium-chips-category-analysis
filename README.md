# Quantium Chips Category Analysis 🔬

## 📌 Project Overview
Completed as part of the Quantium Data Analytics Job Simulation on Forage.
Analysed over 264,000 chip category transactions to identify key customer 
segments, top performing brands and popular pack sizes.

## 🛠️ Tools Used
- R
- RStudio
- ggplot2
- dplyr
- tidyverse

## 📂 Dataset
- Source: Quantium via Forage
- 264,836 transaction records
- Columns: Date, Store Number, Product Name, Quantity, Total Sales,
  Customer Lifestage, Premium Customer Segment

## ❓ Business Questions Answered
1. Which customer segments contribute most to total chip sales?
2. How does average spend differ across customer segments?
3. Which brands dominate chip category sales?
4. Which pack sizes are most popular?

## 📊 Key Findings

### Customer Segments
- Older Families and Young Singles/Couples are the primary revenue drivers
- Older Families average $36 spend per customer — highest of all segments
- Premium customers spend more per transaction regardless of lifestage

### Brand Performance
| Rank | Brand | Total Sales |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Kettle | $390,240 |
| 2 | Smiths | $224,660 |
| 3 | Doritos | $201,539 |
| 4 | Pringles | $177,656 |

### Pack Size Analysis
- 175g and 150g packs are the most popular
- Customers prefer mid-sized packs balancing value and convenience

## 💡 Strategic Recommendations
- Focus marketing on Older Families and Young Singles/Couples
- Increase shelf space for Kettle — dominant brand by a significant margin
- Prioritise stocking 150g–175g pack sizes
- Develop targeted promotions for budget customers using larger pack sizes
